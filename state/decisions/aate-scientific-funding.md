## CIA (Criteria Informativeness Asymmetry)

**CIA is strictly positive in scientific funding:**

Grant review panels structurally use OA metrics as primary selection criteria:
- h-index and publication count are legible, auditable, comparable across proposals
- Journal impact factor provides a rank-ordered OA signal that requires no domain expertise to apply
- Prior grant success creates compounding legibility (funded before → lower evaluator uncertainty → positive prior)
- Citation count provides social-proof OA signal

CA metrics are structurally disadvantaged in panel review:
- Genuine scientific progress cannot be evaluated ex ante — it is inherently prospective and uncertain
- Reviewers assess CA via OA proxies (publication quality, citation count) — but these are already OA instruments
- CA-oriented proposals (high-risk, paradigm-challenging) have higher OA variance → lower expected grant-success OA score under risk-averse panels
- Time pressure in peer review forces reliance on legible OA metrics over CA-evaluating deliberation

**Result**: The review process structurally maximizes CIA by selecting the maximally legible OA metrics for primary use.

## TCA (Training Corrosiveness Amplification)

**TCA confirms strictly across grant selection generations:**

**Generation-level compounding:**
- Cycle n: OA-optimized researcher wins grant → produces publications → higher h-index → larger lab → more publications → higher h-index
- Cycle n+1: OA-optimized researcher enters next competition with amplified OA signals → wins again → cycle deepens
- CA-oriented researchers who did not optimize publication rate have lower h-index → lose competition → reduced funding → reduced capacity for next cycle

**Institutional propagation:**
- Successful OA-optimizing researchers are promoted to senior positions, editorial boards, grant review panels
- Their OA standards become institutionalized as the selection criteria for others
- Department hiring committees use grant success as a primary screen → OA-optimizing researchers selected into faculty positions → next generation trained under OA-optimizing norms

**Cross-generation cultural entrenchment:**
- Labs with OA-optimizing culture produce graduate students socialized into those norms
- Mentorship relationships propagate OA-optimization strategies explicitly (how to frame proposals, which journals to target, how to build h-index)
- Lazonick (2014) compounding mechanism maps directly: just as corporations shifted from "retain-and-reinvest" to "downsize-and-distribute" across competitive generations, academic labs shift from "discover-and-publish" to "publish-and-grant-optimize"

## AATE-Drift Evidence

**Multiple independent empirical records confirm CA degradation while OA metrics hold:**

1. **Replication crisis as AATE-Drift signal** (already in framework as domain #2, but deepens in grant context): most-replicated findings are in domains with lower publication pressure; lowest replication rates in highest-funded, highest-publication-rate domains (psychology, nutrition science) — direct CA–OA anti-correlation in the most OA-optimized subfields

2. **Hyperproductivity–retraction correlation** (Fang & Casadevall 2011): researchers who reported fraud had higher-than-average h-index; Corcoran et al. (2019): high-productivity labs retract at higher rates — CA quality (accuracy) deteriorating while OA metric (publication count) holds

3. **"Hot topics" clustering**: funding concentrates in fashionable research areas even when evidence base is weak (microbiome research 2010s, social priming 2000s, neuroimaging correlates 1990s–2000s) — OA-training (which proposals get funded) shapes research agenda independent of CA-value (genuine knowledge advancement in the area)

4. **Questionable Research Practices (QRPs) prevalence**: John et al. (2012) survey — 94% of researchers admit to at least one QRP (p-hacking, selective reporting, HARKing, optional stopping). These are OA-optimization behaviors executed precisely because grant competition creates OA-training pressure. CA quality (accurate null results, confirmed failures) is sacrificed for OA (publishable positive findings)

5. **Citation gaming**: citation circles, strategic self-citation, citing likely reviewers — pure OA-optimization with zero CA contribution. Empirically documented (Chorus & Waltman 2016)

## CPP (Constitutive Protection Principle) Floor

Grant competition cannot drive CA to zero:
- Funding agencies require some deliverables — a researcher who publishes only fabricated results eventually gets retracted, damaging OA score
- Some researchers maintain CA-orientation despite OA pressure (heterogeneity in the researcher population)
- Paradigm-shifting discoveries occasionally emerge as byproducts of OA-optimized research programs
- Nobel prizes and landmark papers create retroactive OA value for CA-oriented work, partially anchoring the CPP floor

**CPP floor is positive but low**: the proportion of grant-funded research that produces genuine paradigm-shifting CA progress is small relative to the volume of OA-optimized publications.

## Independent Literature Programs

Five independent research programs confirm AATE dynamics in scientific funding without K1-K7:

1. **Goodhart's Law / Campbell's Law** (Goodhart 1975; Campbell 1979): "When a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure." Directly describes h-index, impact factor, citation count becoming grant review targets and subsequently losing CA-tracking value. Pre-existing empirical framework independently deriving AATE-Drift prediction.

2. **Merton's norms vs. actual scientific practice** (Merton 1942; Mitroff 1974; Mulkay 1979): Merton identified communalism, universalism, disinterestedness, organized skepticism as CA-aligned scientific norms. Empirical sociology of science documents systematic deviation — OA-optimization produces counter-Mertonian norms (secrecy, particularism, interest-driven research, credulity toward friendly findings). The documented divergence IS AATE-Drift in sociological language.

3. **Publish-or-perish / bibliometrics literature** (Garfield 1955; Hicks et al. 2015 "Leiden Manifesto"): the Leiden Manifesto explicitly states that bibliometric OA metrics are systematically misaligned with CA scientific quality and warns against using them as primary selection criteria. Published by 14 leading bibliometrics researchers — domain experts identifying the AATE-Drift problem from within the affected system.

4. **Replication crisis research programs** (Ioannidis 2005 "Why Most Published Research Findings Are False"; Open Science Collaboration 2015; Klein et al. 2018 Many Labs): CA degradation (low replication rate) is directly and empirically confirmed in grant-supported scientific domains. Ioannidis (2005) explicitly identifies incentive structures (grant competition, publication pressure) as causal drivers — the formal causal mechanism that AATE names.

5. **Science policy reform literature** (Alberts et al. 2014; Edwards & Roy 2017): explicit calls to reform grant evaluation because OA metrics are crowding out CA scientific quality. These reform proposals presuppose the empirical observation that OA-optimization has degraded CA progress — the pre-theoretical statement of AATE-Drift.

## Ontological Significance

Scientific funding is a **designed system** — but with a distinctive and theoretically important property: the design explicitly **intended to track CA** (genuine scientific progress). Grant review was designed to select the most scientifically valuable proposals. OA metrics (h-index, publications, impact factor) were introduced as **proxies for CA**, not as ends in themselves.

Yet AATE operates anyway.

This makes scientific funding a *stronger* form of evidence than domains where OA was chosen over CA by design:

| Domain | CA-alignment intention | AATE confirmation |
|---|---|---|
| AI RLHF [C55] | Partial — behavioral compliance was meant to proxy alignment | Confirmed |
| Replication crisis [C56] | High — publication was meant to reflect truth | Confirmed |
| Educational assessment [C56] | High — tests were meant to measure understanding | Confirmed |
| Evolutionary epistemology [C59] | None — selection has no CA-intention | Confirmed |
| Market competition [C60] | Partial — profit meant to proxy social value creation | Confirmed |
| **Scientific funding [C61]** | **Maximum — explicit CA-alignment mandate, institutional design, peer review mechanism** | **Confirmed** |

Scientific funding has the strongest CA-alignment intention of any confirmed domain. AATE operating despite maximum design effort to prevent it constitutes strong evidence that AATE is robust to institutional countermeasures — a structural feature of optimization dynamics, not a design failure.
