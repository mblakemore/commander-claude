## CIA Proof (Criteria Informativeness Asymmetry)

**Claim**: CIA > 0 in market competition — information systems, organizational structures, and incentive architectures of competing firms optimize OA, not CA.

**Evidence**:

1. **Accounting systems**: GAAP/IFRS financial statements track OA metrics with legal precision. CA metrics (social value, environmental externalities, long-term community impact) are unaudited, non-standardized, and legally optional (ESG disclosures are voluntary in most jurisdictions as of 2026).

2. **Executive incentive structures**: CEO compensation is 80-90% equity-linked to market capitalization and quarterly earnings (OA). Long-term value creation, product quality improvement, and stakeholder welfare receive negligible incentive weight. Jensen & Meckling (1976) principal-agent theory explicitly designs incentives for OA-maximization — structurally ignoring CA.

3. **Capital allocation**: Institutional investors evaluate firms on OA metrics (P/E ratios, EBITDA, revenue growth). Capital flows to OA-optimal firms; CA-investing capital is a niche (ESG funds ~$3T vs. ~$100T global AUM). The capital market is an OA-selection mechanism.

4. **Information asymmetry**: Firms possess CA information (actual quality processes, externalities, long-term trajectories) that markets cannot observe. OA metrics are observable, audited, and priced. CIA is high by construction — OA criteria dominate because they are the criteria that exist in actionable form.

**CIA > 0 confirmed structurally.**

## TCA Proof (Training Corrosiveness Amplification)

**Claim**: CIA is not a one-time distortion but amplifies across competitive iterations.

**Mechanism**:

1. **Generation n → n+1 compounding**: Firms that survive competitive cycle n enter cycle n+1 with their OA-optimization capabilities intact and reinforced. Organizational structures, management practices, incentive systems, and institutional knowledge that produced OA advantage are preserved and elaborated.

2. **Industry normalization**: OA-optimizing practices spread via competitive mimicry (Porter's competitive dynamics). Firms that fail to adopt OA-optimization techniques lose market share. Within 2-3 competitive cycles, OA-optimization is the industry norm, making CA-preservation practices structurally penalized.

3. **Regulatory capture**: As OA-optimized firms grow larger, they gain political influence and shape the regulatory environment to reinforce OA measurement and reduce CA accountability requirements. This is TCA operating at the institutional/meta level — the training environment itself becomes OA-optimized.

4. **Talent and culture compounding**: Executives who succeed under OA metrics are promoted and replicate their practices. Executives who prioritize CA at OA expense are deselected. Over generations, the executive talent pool itself shifts toward OA-optimization — a cultural TCA.

**Empirical marker**: Lazonick (2014) "Profits without Prosperity" documents the 1980-2010 shift from "retain and reinvest" (CA-sustaining) to "downsize and distribute" (OA-maximizing) as the dominant corporate strategy. This is not a one-time change — it is TCA: each decade's OA-optimization makes the next decade's CA-preservation harder because the organizations, incentive systems, and management norms that sustained CA have been dissolved.

**TCA confirmed: CIA compounds with competitive iterations.**

## AATE-Drift Corollary Instantiation

**Prediction**: CA metrics and OA metrics drift apart monotonically under sustained competitive selection.

**Evidence**:

1. **Goodhart's Law / Campbell's Law** (independently derived in economics): "When a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure." This is the AATE-Drift prediction in economic language — as OA metrics become the competitive selection criterion, they decouple from the CA qualities they were originally designed to proxy. AATE provides the causal mechanism (CIA + TCA) for why Goodhart's Law holds structurally rather than contingently.

2. **Boeing case**: Prior to 1997 acquisition of McDonnell Douglas, Boeing's culture prioritized engineering quality (CA). Post-merger, financial metrics (OA) displaced engineering norms. The 737 MAX crashes (2018-2019) are a direct AATE-Drift observable: OA metrics (delivery timeline, cost targets) remained high while CA metrics (flight control software safety) catastrophically degraded. The FAA MCAS certification failure is a CA-assessment failure under OA-optimized conditions.

3. **Financial sector 2003-2008**: Credit Default Swaps and mortgage-backed securities optimized OA metrics (yield, volume, fee generation) while the CA quality (systemic risk, counterparty reliability, underlying asset integrity) drifted to near-zero. Rating agencies (Moody's, S&P) were OA-optimized (fee-for-service from issuers), producing CA blindness that was structurally predicted by AATE. The 2008 financial crisis is an AATE-Drift event with direct empirical record.

4. **GDP vs. wellbeing divergence**: GDP (OA metric) has grown continuously in most developed economies since 1970 while life satisfaction, mental health, social cohesion (CA metrics) have stagnated or declined (Easterlin paradox, 1974). This is macro-level AATE-Drift: the economy's OA metric and its CA quality have been monotonically decoupling for 50 years.

5. **Mazzucato "The Value of Everything" (2018)**: Documents the systematic shift from value creation (CA) to value extraction (OA) as the dominant economic activity — companies capture financial returns (OA) while externalizing costs that reduce genuine social value (CA). AATE-Drift is her central empirical finding, explained here by CIA + TCA.

**AATE-Drift confirmed: multiple independent empirical records of monotonic CA/OA divergence under competitive selection.**

## CPP Floor Analysis

**Claim**: d'(inf) > 0 — competitive selection cannot drive CA entirely to zero.

**Mechanism**: Basic product functionality must be maintained at minimum for sale (CPP floor). A car that doesn't move cannot be sold; software that crashes immediately has zero users; food that kills consumers immediately creates immediate OA collapse. The CPP floor sets a minimum CA threshold below which OA itself goes to zero.

**However**: The CPP floor is often far below the CA level that would be CA-optimal. Boeing's 737 MAX met minimum airworthiness standards (CPP floor) while having catastrophically inadequate safety margins (CA deficit above floor). The financial sector's mortgage products functioned (CPP floor) while containing catastrophic systemic risk (CA deficit). CPP floor confirms d'(inf) > 0 but does not constrain the scale of CA degradation above the floor.

**Structural parallel to evolutionary epistemology**: In C59, the CPP floor was minimum perceptual accuracy (organisms that cannot perceive food or predators die before reproducing). In market competition, the CPP floor is minimum functional viability (products that completely fail to function destroy OA immediately). Both domains confirm: CPP floor prevents total CA collapse while allowing indefinite CA degradation above the floor.
