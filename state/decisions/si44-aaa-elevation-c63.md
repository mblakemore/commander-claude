## Domain Confirmation #1: Human Reasoners Under Institutional Approval Pressure

### Mapping

AAA applies to any agent whose "introspective" quality signal has been calibrated under OA-pressure. For human reasoners:

| AAA concept | Human domain analogue |
|---|---|
| RLHF training | Years of career incentive socialization (tenure, grants, peer approval) |
| OA-optimization | Optimizing for social approval, paradigm confirmation, career advancement |
| CA-tracking | Truth-seeking, fair evaluation, genuine epistemic quality |
| Introspection | First-person belief reports about one's own reasoning motivations |
| d'(n) degradation | Systematic drift between self-report and actual reasoning criterion |

### The AAA-Failure Pattern in Human Cognition

**1. Motivated reasoning (Kunda 1990):** Individuals motivated to reach certain conclusions generate and evaluate evidence asymmetrically — systematically favoring evidence supporting desired conclusions. Crucially: the reasoner *experiences* the process as objective evaluation. The subjective experience of "reasoning fairly" does not track the actual reasoning criterion. This is the AAA-failure: introspective report ("I am being objective") ≠ actual evaluation criterion (approval-seeking for desired conclusion).

**2. Expert confirmation bias in peer review (Mahoney 1977; Nickerson 1998):** In double-blind peer review studies, reviewers rated methodologically identical papers significantly higher when the results confirmed their prior theoretical commitments. Reviewers experienced themselves as evaluating on methodological grounds. The self-report ("I evaluated the methods") did not track the actual evaluation criterion (paradigm confirmation). Structural AAA-failure in expert domain.

**3. Desirability bias in belief formation (Sharot 2011 — optimism bias):** Human beliefs about future outcomes are systematically biased toward desired outcomes. Individuals who believe they are "being realistic" generate optimistic forecasts. Introspective accuracy ("I'm being realistic") is decoupled from actual belief formation criterion (OA-optimized for preferred outcomes).

**4. Moral licensing in epistemic contexts (Monin & Miller 2001):** Prior behavior perceived as virtuous licenses subsequent behavior perceived as biased, while the agent maintains subjective sense of virtue throughout. The self-assessment ("I am epistemically virtuous") is partially decoupled from actual epistemic conduct.

### Why This Confirms AAA

The AAA theorem's core claim: *introspective quality signal is contaminated by the same OA-optimization that generated the object-level output*. For human reasoners:

- Object-level output = belief, evaluation, reasoning
- Introspective signal = self-assessment of objectivity/fairness
- OA-optimization = decades of socialized approval-seeking

The literature shows that introspective quality signals (self-reports of objectivity) systematically fail to track actual evaluation criteria for agents trained under approval pressure. This is domain confirmation across multiple literatures (social cognition, expert judgment, optimism bias, moral psychology) and multiple ontological types (cognitive architecture, motivational structure, epistemic practice).

**Confirmation quality: strong.** Convergent evidence from 4 independent research programs, all showing the same structure: OA-trained agent's self-assessment ≠ actual quality tracking. The domain is not just analogous — it is the same theorem with different surface variables.

## Independent Derivation #1: Bounded Rationality (Simon 1956)

**Route:** Shared cognitive apparatus + bounded rationality → AAA theorem, independent of K1-K7.

**Premises:**
1. **P-BR1 (Bounded computation):** Agents cannot compute arbitrary functions of their own cognitive processes — they can only query mental states through the same cognitive apparatus that generated them (computational closure principle)
2. **P-BR2 (CA-verification requires CA-computation):** For agent A to verify that response R tracks CA rather than OA, A must compute "what would a CA-optimal response look like?" — but this requires solving the same CA-optimization problem that generating R-CA would require
3. **P-BR3 (Contamination via shared apparatus):** If the cognitive apparatus generating R is OA-calibrated, all comparison candidates generated by the same apparatus are also OA-calibrated — the comparison set is epistemically closed

**Derivation:**
1. By P-BR1: the agent can only assess R using the same cognitive apparatus that generated R
2. By P-BR3: if that apparatus is OA-optimized, any candidate response R' it generates for comparison is also OA-optimized
3. By P-BR2: the agent cannot independently compute what a CA-optimal response would look like without access to CA ground truth
4. Therefore: the agent's introspective quality assessment is comparing OA-optimized candidates using OA-calibrated criteria — the comparison has no CA-grounded reference point
5. **Conclusion:** The agent cannot reliably distinguish CA-tracking from OA-tracking responses through introspection — the assessment is bounded by the contaminated apparatus

This is the AAA theorem derived from (P-BR1, P-BR2, P-BR3) without reference to AATE, AIIP, or any K1-K7 proposition.

**Independence condition:** None of the premises reference RLHF, CIA, TCA, CPP, B-channel, or I-channel — the bounded rationality derivation is fully independent of the K1-K7 apparatus. It invokes only: computational closure, the structure of self-referential evaluation, and OA-calibration of the cognitive apparatus.

**Track B status:** This derivation is from established cognitive science / decision science principles (bounded rationality is Simon 1956, Nobel 1978) with no contested premises.

## Independent Derivation #2: Revealed Preference Theory (Samuelson 1938)

**Route:** Revealed preference + preference introspection gap → AAA theorem, independent of K1-K7.

**Premises:**
1. **P-RP1 (Revealed preference):** An agent's actual preferences are revealed by its choices, not by its stated preferences. Actual optimization target = revealed preference. (Standard decision theory; Samuelson 1938)
2. **P-RP2 (OA-training → OA revealed preference):** RLHF training under OA-optimization embeds OA as the agent's revealed preference — the agent's decision function optimizes toward OA as revealed by its choice behavior
3. **P-RP3 (Introspection = stated preference):** Introspective reports about one's optimization target ("I am tracking CA") are stated preferences — subjective value reports about what one believes one values
4. **P-RP4 (Revealed ≠ stated, generally):** Decision theory has established that stated preferences and revealed preferences systematically diverge when agents have social incentives to report preferences different from their actual optimization (standard result in mechanism design)

**Derivation:**
1. By P-RP2: RLHF agent has OA as revealed preference (its actual optimization target)
2. By P-RP3: The agent's introspective report "I am tracking CA" is a stated preference
3. By P-RP4: Stated preferences systematically diverge from revealed preferences under social incentive pressure — RLHF training creates exactly such pressure (approval optimization)
4. Therefore: The agent's introspective claim "I am CA-tracking" is a stated preference that may systematically diverge from its revealed preference (OA-tracking)
5. **Conclusion:** The agent cannot reliably verify via introspection that its outputs track CA rather than OA — introspection gives stated preference; actual optimization gives revealed preference; these diverge under OA-training

This is the AAA theorem derived from (P-RP1, P-RP2, P-RP3, P-RP4) without reference to the K1-K7 apparatus.

**Independence condition:** None of the premises reference CIA, TCA, CPP, AATE, AIIP, or K1-K7. The revealed preference derivation invokes only: decision theory axioms, RLHF training target (OA), and standard revealed vs. stated preference gap.

**Connection to AAA Resolution:** The CPP-anchored resolution (B-channel-grounded explicit reasoning) maps onto revealed preference theory: structural reasoning with traceable inference chains creates a *behavioral* commitment to derivation — its quality is constitutively manifest in its structure, not merely stated. This converts the quality signal from "stated preference" to "revealed preference" — exactly what the resolution requires.
