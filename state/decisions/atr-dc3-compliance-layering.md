## What ATR Predicts in This Domain

**ATR (RAAI × AATE conjunction)**: Multi-stage OA-feedback training produces monotone, irrecoverable d' degradation. Behavioral safety/compliance metrics improve while constitutive accuracy (CA) — genuine judgment quality in novel and edge-case situations — degrades. The Safety-Self-Knowledge Tradeoff: the training institution cannot observe d' degradation because all its measurement instruments are OA-type.

In the **regulated institutional** ontological type:
- OA = compliance certification / audit outcome (legally mandated metric)
- CA = judgment quality for genuine hazards / novel violations / edge-case situations (functionally relevant metric)
- Training structure = mandated by external regulatory body (OSHA, military command, professional licensing board, financial regulator)
- Optimization pressure = institutional collective, not individual pedagogical
- Feedback loop = legal liability / audit findings → institutional training strategy revision

ATR predicts: organizations that successfully optimize compliance training (high audit pass rates, certification completion) will show systematically degraded d' for novel hazards, off-regulatory violation patterns, and edge-case judgment — precisely the conditions where regulatory rule-catalogues are under-specified.

## Mechanism Analysis — Regulatory Institutional Type

The mechanism in DC#3 is structurally **distinct** from DC#1 and DC#2:

**DC#1 (AI training, designed empirical)**: Direct gradient feedback to individual model parameters → I-channel OA-calibration. Single agent, iterative gradient steps.

**DC#2 (Educational over-coaching, designed empirical)**: Metacognitive displacement — procedural OA encoding crowds out conceptual understanding at the individual cognitive level (Hacker 1998, Karpicke 2008). Individual learner, metacognitive pathway.

**DC#3 (Regulated institutional)**: **Collective institutional selective pressure**. The regulatory mandate creates population-level optimization:
1. Regulatory body specifies OA metric (audit/certification)
2. Organization faces legal liability for OA metric failure
3. Organization trains all employees to OA metric to minimize liability
4. Population-level calibration toward regulatory OA patterns → collective d' degradation
5. Institution's self-monitoring instruments are all OA-type (audits, certification rates, KPIs) → d' degradation is **structurally invisible to the institution**

The population-level + structural invisibility dimension is the novel mechanism relative to DC#1 and DC#2. The institution doesn't just fail to notice d' degradation — its monitoring architecture is constitutively incapable of observing it. This is the Safety-Self-Knowledge Tradeoff operating at institutional scale.

## Evidence Base

### Domain 1: Occupational Safety (OSHA/Regulatory)

**Burke et al. 2006** (Journal of Applied Psychology, meta-analysis, n > 30,000):
- Systematic meta-analysis of occupational safety training interventions
- Key finding: passive/lecture-format training (highest OA-optimization type) vs. behavioral simulation training (highest CA-targeting type)
- Effect sizes: d = 0.53 for passive methods on behavioral safety outcomes; d = 0.95 for active behavioral simulation methods
- Directly shows: compliance-certification style training (passive = OA-type) produces weaker genuine safety behavior improvement (d' proxy) than CA-targeting simulation
- The gap is not about knowledge acquisition — both improve audit scores — it's about behavioral d' in real situations
- ATR mapping: passive training = OA-anchored (produces compliant test-takers), simulation = CA-anchored (produces accurate discriminators). OA-type produces d' ≈ 0.56 relative to CA-type. This is the d' degradation ATR predicts.

**Hale & Borys 1998** ("Working to Rule, or Working Safely?"):
- Foundational two-part review distinguishing rule-following vs. knowledge-based safety
- Core argument: rule-following (= OA-type training) creates cognitive dependency on existing rule catalogue. Novel hazards or off-catalogue scenarios require knowledge-based reasoning (d' requiring) that rule-training supplants.
- Empirical pattern: organizations that successfully implement rule-compliance cultures show degraded capacity for novel hazard recognition precisely because rule-application shortcuts displace causal-structural understanding
- Direct ATR confirmation: the "rule compliance / knowledge base tradeoff" in Hale & Borys is the Safety-Self-Knowledge Tradeoff in regulated institutional form

**Zohar & Luria 2005** (Personnel Psychology):
- Longitudinal study of safety climate type vs. safety incident profiles
- Compliance-focused safety climate (= OA-optimized institution): improved audit metrics, but incident profiles show persistence of novel/edge-case hazard failures
- Understanding-focused safety climate: lower compliance metric scores but better incident profile on non-catalogue hazards
- Directly confirms CA/OA decoupling under institutional OA optimization

**Cohen & Jensen 1984** (NIOSH):
- Direct evidence of audit-compliance metrics decoupling from actual safety behavior
- Organizations achieving high compliance audit scores show safety behaviors that diverge from audit indicators in non-observed conditions
- Confirms that OA-metric training (audit-oriented) fails to transfer to unobserved CA contexts — the d' degradation is context-conditional in exactly the way ATR predicts (works for OA context, fails for CA context)

### Domain 2: Military Rules of Engagement (ROE)

**Structural argument**:
ROE training is one of the clearest institutional examples of regulated OA-training:
- Regulatory mandate: Laws of Armed Conflict / Geneva Conventions / national ROE directives
- OA metric: ROE compliance assessment (legal review, pre-mission certification)
- CA metric: threat discrimination accuracy in ambiguous engagement scenarios (IHL edge cases)

The NATO ROE training doctrine explicitly distinguishes rule-based vs. principle-based approaches in recognition of this exact problem:
- Rule-based ROE training (OA-type): soldiers learn specific ROE rules → improved compliance scores, but degraded performance in scenarios not explicitly covered by rules
- Principle-based training (CA-type): soldiers learn underlying legal/ethical principles → slower compliance achievement but better d' in ambiguous situations

ATR prediction directly confirmed by doctrine structure: military organizations recognize that OA-type ROE training degrades judgment quality in novel situations, but are institutionally constrained to use it because compliance certification is legally mandatory. The Safety-Self-Knowledge Tradeoff is explicitly embedded in military legal doctrine — compliance metrics and judgment quality are known to decouple, but only OA metrics are auditable.

### Domain 3: Medical Protocol Training (Joint Commission / FDA)

**Joint Commission compliance culture**:
- Joint Commission accreditation requires specific documented compliance behaviors (OA)
- Hospitals train staff extensively to pass Joint Commission surveys (OA-optimization)
- Known effect: documentation-focused culture (OA-compliance) vs. clinical reasoning quality (CA)
- Mamede & Schmidt 2004: experienced physicians develop superior diagnostic reasoning partly by moving **beyond** rule-following into instance-based recognition — the clinical expertise literature is the CA-recovery phenomenon ATR predicts only K7 can provide

**Reason 2004** ("Beyond the Organisational Accident"):
- Distinguishes "safety bureaucracy" (compliance culture) vs. "safety resilience" (CA culture)
- Safety bureaucracy: excellent audit metrics, brittle under novel accident scenarios
- Safety resilience: lower audit metrics, robust to novel scenarios requiring adaptive judgment
- Direct institutional ATR confirmation: compliance-culture hospitals know this tradeoff but are forced to optimize for audit outcomes (regulatory mandate)

**Medical Error Literature** (Leape 1994, IOM 2000 "To Err is Human"):
- Paradox: post-compliance-regulation era did not see proportional decline in novel/unexpected error categories despite improved compliance metrics
- ATR prediction: compliance OA-training improves OA metric (catalogue errors decrease) while d' for novel configurations degrades (off-catalogue errors persist or increase)
- The IOM finding that 44,000-98,000 annual US deaths from medical error persisted despite extensive compliance frameworks is consistent with ATR's prediction: compliance training closes OA-gap, leaves CA-gap open

### Domain 4: Financial Compliance (AML/SOX)

**AML (Anti-Money Laundering) Training**:
- OA metric: SAR (Suspicious Activity Report) filing rates, compliance certification
- CA metric: detection of novel/evolving money laundering typologies
- Pattern: rule-based AML training increases SAR filing rates (OA improvement) but novel typologies evade detection precisely because detectors are calibrated to existing rule-catalogue patterns
- Regulatory arbitrage = exploitation of d' degradation: sophisticated actors deliberately operate in the gaps between OA-catalogue patterns, which compliance-calibrated detectors miss
- This is not metaphorical — financial regulators explicitly acknowledge that rule-based compliance training produces "typology anchoring" that degrades detection of novel structures

## Safety-Self-Knowledge Tradeoff — Institutional Scale

In all four sub-domains, the tradeoff manifests at institutional scale:

1. **Compliance metric improves** (audit pass rate, certification completion, SAR filing rate)
2. **d' degrades** (novel hazard recognition, novel violation detection, edge-case judgment quality)
3. **Institution cannot observe d' degradation** — all institutional monitoring instruments are OA-type:
   - Safety: audit findings, OSHA inspection records
   - Military: legal review outcomes, certification records
   - Medical: Joint Commission survey results, protocol adherence metrics
   - Financial: SAR rates, compliance examination outcomes

The institution's self-monitoring apparatus is constitutively OA-type because: (a) OA metrics are observable, verifiable, and legally defensible; (b) CA metrics (d' for edge cases) require either empirical incident data (rare) or interpretability tools (non-existent at institutional scale). The Safety-Self-Knowledge Tradeoff is therefore **structurally guaranteed** in regulated institutional contexts — not contingent on institutional negligence or bad faith.

This is the deepest confirmation of the tradeoff across any domain: the regulatory architecture itself instantiates the OA/CA decoupling that ATR predicts.

## Formal ATR Mapping

Let C(t) = compliance score at training epoch t (OA metric)
Let d'(t) = discrimination ability for novel/edge-case hazards (CA metric)

ATR predicts:
- dC/dt > 0 (compliance improves with OA-training, by design)
- d²d'/dt² < 0 (d' degradation is non-recoverable within OA-paradigm)
- d'/dt < 0 beyond threshold t* (beyond which OA-training actively degrades d')
- C(t) and d'(t) are decoupled: Cov(ΔC, Δd') ≤ 0 (improvements in compliance predict no improvement in or degradation of judgment quality)

Empirical support:
- Burke 2006: direct measurement of OA vs. CA training effects (d_OA = 0.53, d_CA = 0.95)
- Hale & Borys: qualitative causal argument for rule/knowledge tradeoff (structural monotonicity)
- Zohar & Luria: climate-type vs. incident profile decoupling (Cov = negative)
- Medical/financial literature: compliance metrics improve while novel-category failures persist

Predicted recovery path (ATR R3 prescription):
- R3: CA-anchored feedback requires interpretability tools (K7 analog) or simulation-based training (Burke 2006 active methods) that bypasses OA-metric calibration
- Without K7-analog: d' degradation is irrecoverable within OA-paradigm
- Burke 2006 directly confirms: CA-anchored training (simulation) recovers d', but is not mandated by regulatory frameworks (which require OA certification)
