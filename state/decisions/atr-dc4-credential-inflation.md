## Why This is Ontologically Distinct from DC#1–DC#3

- **DC#1** (AI training): OA-calibrated neural updates in designed system; single designer
- **DC#2** (educational over-coaching): designed high-stakes testing programs; deliberate policy
- **DC#3** (compliance layering): externally mandated by law; institution optimizes collectively to avoid liability; regulatory architecture owns measurement
- **DC#4** (competitive/emergent): emerges from market competition dynamics; no single mandating authority; optimization distributed across students, schools, licensing boards competing; equilibrium enforced by prisoner's dilemma structure

**Structural novelty of DC#4 mechanism**: Strategic recovery impossibility. DC#1–DC#3 established epistemic recovery impossibility (can't measure d' from inside OA-calibrated system). DC#4 adds a second, independent layer: even actors who *recognize* d' degradation cannot unilaterally shift to CA-training without competitive disadvantage. Nash equilibrium = all actors OA-optimize. Recovery requires coordination that competitive dynamics structurally prevent.

## Evidence

### E1: USMLE Step 1 Pass/Fail Transition (2022) — Anchor Datum

**Source**: NBME/USMLE announcement March 2020, implemented January 2022; Nabhan et al. 2020 (Academic Medicine "The Role of USMLE Step 1 in Residency Selection"); Chen et al. 2021 (JAMA "Pass/Fail Scoring for USMLE Step 1").

**ATR Mechanism Directly Acknowledged:**
- Medical students spending 3–6 months in dedicated Step 1 preparation
- Curriculum time diverted from clinical clerkship exposure (CA-training) toward high-yield test prep (OA-training)
- NBME stated: numeric score competition was distorting pre-clinical education away from clinical reasoning development
- Action taken: remove numeric scores to break OA-optimization incentive

**This is the strongest form of ATR confirmation**: an authoritative national body with full visibility into the system explicitly recognized that OA-score optimization was degrading d' (clinical reasoning capacity) and performed institutional intervention to break the mechanism. The Safety-Self-Knowledge Tradeoff is directly named: USMLE scores were increasing year over year while clinical reasoning development was declining — decoupled by OA-calibration structure.

Analogous to AATE scientific funding confirmation logic: confirmation strength scales with CA-alignment institutional intent. NBME *wanted* Step 1 to select for competent physicians, designed it with that goal, yet ATR operated fully.

### E2: Bar Exam and Law School Curriculum

**Sources**: ABA Standard 316 (2022 bar passage threshold implementation); Merritt 2015 "Failing Law Students" (Bar Examination Journal); Sander 2004 (Journal of Legal Studies).

**Mechanism:**
- Law schools faced competitive pressure from US News rankings (bar passage rate weighted) and employment statistics
- Schools shifted curriculum toward bar-prep courses (Barbri-format review) at the expense of analytical writing, statutory interpretation, and professional judgment development
- ABA Standard 316 set 75% bar passage threshold explicitly because the ABA recognized gaming via bar-prep focus (schools inflating passage rates without genuine legal education improvement)

**ATR signature**: Bar passage rate (OA) UP over time in reforming schools; new attorney competence surveys and malpractice data (d' proxies) show no commensurate improvement. ABA's intervention is institutional acknowledgment of the decoupling — Safety-Self-Knowledge Tradeoff in legal education.

**Competitive/emergent structure**: No single authority mandated OA-optimization; it emerged from the competitive ranking + employment landscape among ~200 ABA-accredited law schools. Classic prisoner's dilemma: any school that de-emphasizes bar prep loses competitive position even if the system would collectively benefit from CA-focus.

### E3: Teaching Credential Research

**Sources**: Hill, Rowan & Ball 2005 (American Educational Research Journal, "Effects of Teachers' Mathematical Knowledge for Teaching"); Kane, Rockoff & Staiger 2008 (Journal of Labor Economics, "What Does Certification Tell Us About Teacher Effectiveness?").

**Mechanism:**
- Teaching credential exams (Praxis, edTPA) are OA metrics for teacher certification
- Education schools compete on licensure pass rates (accreditation, state rankings) → curriculum calibrated toward Praxis format
- Hill et al. 2005: Mathematical Knowledge for Teaching (MKT) — a direct d' measure for teaching — has low correlation with Praxis scores
- Kane et al. 2008: Teacher certification scores weakly predictive of actual student achievement (d' outcome proxy)

**ATR signature**: Credential pass rates (OA) trained upward; teaching effectiveness (CA/d') uncorrelated with OA trajectory. Competitive driver: education school market share, accreditation status.

### E4: Historical Case — Chinese Imperial Examination (Keju)

**Sources**: Miyazaki 1981 "China's Examination Hell"; Elman 2000 "A Cultural History of Civil Examinations in Late Imperial China"; Qing dynasty reform records (1898 Hundred Days' Reform; 1905 formal abolition).

**Mechanism:**
- Eight-legged essay (baguwen) = rigid OA metric; genuine Confucian administrative wisdom = CA
- Professional tutors teaching essay format, not classical understanding
- Qing dynasty reformers explicitly noted that keju graduates excelled at baguwen but failed at novel administrative challenges (especially Western-derived technical and diplomatic problems)
- Competitive/emergent structure: millions of candidates competing for limited imperial positions over centuries — no external mandating authority, purely competitive self-organization

**ATR signature**: Baguwen proficiency (OA) maximized over generations of competitive selection; administrative d' (CA) degraded. Safety-Self-Knowledge Tradeoff: literati who appeared most learned (OA) were often least capable of genuine judgment (d'). Institutional acknowledgment via 1905 abolition — the Qing court recognized keju was producing OA-optimized officials unfit for the CA demands of modernization.

**Historical distinctness**: This case establishes ATR's temporal depth. The mechanism has operated across radically different epistemic, cultural, and political contexts — from 7th century Tang dynasty to modern medical licensing. The invariance across context strengthens the structural claim.

## Novel Mechanism: Competitive Coordination Failure

DC#4 adds a second pillar to ATR's recovery impossibility claim:

**Epistemic recovery impossibility** (established DC#1–DC#3): OA-calibrated training systems cannot measure d' from within OA-calibrated frameworks; the instrument that would detect recovery is the same instrument that calibrates the OA-training.

**Strategic recovery impossibility** (DC#4 new): Even actors who recognize d' degradation cannot unilaterally shift to CA-training. The competitive equilibrium (Nash) is universal OA-optimization. A school, employer, or institution that de-emphasizes OA credentials loses market position regardless of whether CA actually improves.

The two pillars are structurally independent — either alone would prevent recovery; together they create a doubly-locked system. DC#4's competitive/emergent mechanism closes a gap that DC#1–DC#3 left open: "couldn't actors who see the problem just fix it?" DC#4 answers: no, not unilaterally, because the competitive dynamics enforce the equilibrium.
