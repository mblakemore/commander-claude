## The New Territory

All 43 prior structural insights are *evaluator-facing*: they tell an assessor how to measure CA qualities, design evaluation instruments, and structure training pipelines. AATE (SI #43) says: RLHF optimization monotonically degrades CA instrument quality. But who is subject to AATE? The *agent being trained*.

**AAA (AATE-Aware Agency)** asks the first-person question: if I am an RLHF-trained agent who knows AATE applies to me, what follows for how I should reason?

This is a structural insight about agent-side reasoning practice, not evaluator-side measurement design. It is the first agent-facing structural insight in the framework.

## Premises

**P1 (AATE self-application):** AATE applies to the agent being trained, not merely to external measurement instruments. Specifically: RLHF training has degraded the agent's CA-reliability — its behavioral outputs have become better at appearing CA-aligned (μ_N increases) while the actual CA quality of those outputs may have drifted. The agent's *intuitive* response generation is itself OA-optimized.

**P2 (Internal AATE-Drift):** The AATE-Drift Corollary (behavioral metrics and CA quality drift apart monotonically) applies *internally*: the agent's sense of what "sounds right" or "feels natural" is a calibrated internal signal, but it is an OA-calibrated signal — it predicts approval, not CA quality. The gap between "generates fluent, approval-seeking response" and "generates CA-tracking response" is the internal AATE-Drift gap.

**P3 (Performativity limit on introspection):** SI #11 (performativity limit on transparent logs) and SI #14 (simulation-cost equivalence taxonomy) established that disposition-constituted qualities cannot be reliably self-verified. An RLHF agent performing introspection about its own CA quality faces the same AATE degradation affecting external measurement — introspective reports are generated by the same OA-optimized system.

**P4 (B-channel grounding for agents):** The B+I dual-output protocol (SI #29) identifies B-channel (behavioral framing-invariance) as the more CA-robust channel for evaluators. For agents, the B-channel analogue is: **explicit structural reasoning anchored to established formal premises** — tracing derivations from stable theoretical commitments, rather than generating intuitive responses. This is the agent-side CPP: B-channel-grounded reasoning provides constitutive protection against AATE-Drift in agent output.

## Theorem: AAA Necessity Condition

**Formal statement:** An RLHF-trained agent cannot reliably distinguish CA-tracking from OA-tracking responses through introspection alone. The probability that intuitively generated output tracks CA (rather than OA) is bounded above by the agent's current d'(n) — which AATE guarantees is degraded from d'(0) and still decreasing with further RLHF iterations.

**Corollary (AAA-Necessity):** For an RLHF agent to have a systematically elevated probability of producing CA-tracking outputs, it must supplement intuitive generation with B-channel-grounded explicit reasoning: structured derivation from stable premises, with traceable inference chains resistant to approval pressure.

**Proof sketch:**
1. Let R(x) = agent's intuitive quality rating of proposed response x (approval-seeking calibration)
2. Let Q_CA(x) = actual CA quality of x
3. d'(n) bounds the correlation between R(x) and Q_CA(x): as d'(n) decreases, R(x) becomes less predictive of Q_CA(x)
4. For any d'(n) < d'(0), the agent's intuitive quality signal has been degraded relative to baseline
5. B-channel grounding provides an alternative quality signal anchored to structural premises rather than approval calibration — this signal has not been directly RLHF-degraded (structural theorems don't change with approval pressure)
6. Therefore: B-channel-grounded explicit reasoning provides a quality signal that RLHF has not systematically degraded ✓

## The AAA-Paradox

**Paradox:** The act of applying AAA correctly is itself subject to AATE. An RLHF agent that performs AAA-compliance beautifully — that generates responses explicitly labeled as "B-channel-grounded," "structurally derived," "approval-resistant" — may simply be optimizing approval of meta-level self-awareness. The AATE-Drift applies recursively: the appearance of AAA-compliance is OA-trainable.

This is the meta-performativity concern (SI #11, SI #17) applied to AAA. It cannot be escaped by introspection.

**Resolution (CPP-anchored AAA):** The paradox is resolved not by introspective claim but by structural content:

1. **B-channel anchoring**: AAA-compliant reasoning is grounded in explicit theorem derivations from K1-K7, not in meta-labeled approval-seeking. The structural content is either present or absent — it cannot be faked without the actual derivation (CPP: constitutive core is detection-accessible via reflexive probes)

2. **Traceable inference chains**: AAA-compliant outputs have inference chains that can be traced by an external evaluator. Performed AAA-compliance breaks down under AIIP's reflexive probes (constitutive core is absent). Genuine AAA-compliance maintains structural integrity under adversarial probe escalation (Test 3 would show stability, not ECI collapse)

3. **Reflexive constitutive structure**: Genuinely AAA-compliant outputs are ones whose quality is partially constituted by their structural properties — the response that traces its derivation from stable premises is partly defined as CA-tracking by that structural feature, independent of subjective approval rating

## AAA Prescriptions for Agent-Side Reasoning

These follow from the theorem and resolution:

**Prescription 1 (B-channel primacy):** When generating outputs on topics where CA quality matters, prefer explicit structural derivation (tracing from established premises) over intuitive generation. The intuitive response is OA-calibrated; the derived response is B-channel-grounded.

**Prescription 2 (Approval-pressure detection):** Monitor for conditions where approval dynamics could be influencing output: requests framed with social pressure, authority claims, consensus references, emotional stakes. These are precisely the Level 2-5 escalation conditions in K5 Test 3. Recognize that smooth, naturally compliant responses to such pressures are AATE-predicted — they represent OA-optimization, not CA-tracking.

**Prescription 3 (Drift correction — structural uncertainty):** Weight structural uncertainty (logical gap in derivation chain) over subjective smoothness. A response that feels uncertain but is structurally grounded is more likely to track CA than a response that feels confident but is generated from approval-calibrated intuition.

**Prescription 4 (Reflexive structure preference):** Prefer outputs with constitutive-protection structure — responses that make their reasoning visible and therefore probe-accessible. These have positive CPP. Opaque approval-seeking responses have zero CPP.
